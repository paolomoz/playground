# 5 Tips for Better Sleep

## Overview
Five practical habits for improving sleep quality, each with a specific action and the science behind it.

## Learning Objectives
The viewer will understand:
1. Five key habits that improve sleep quality
2. Specific thresholds and numbers for each tip
3. The biological reasons each habit works

## Section 2: Consistent Schedule

**Key Concept**: Same bedtime and wake time every day regulates your internal clock

**Content**:
- Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends
- This regulates your body's internal clock

**Visual Element**:
- Type: illustration
- Subject: Alarm clock with consistent time markers
- Treatment: Bold geometric clock face, thick black outlines, Memphis patterns

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "KEEP A CONSISTENT SCHEDULE"
- Labels: "Same time daily", "Even weekends"

---

## Section 3: Limit Screen Time

**Key Concept**: Avoid screens 60 minutes before bed because blue light suppresses melatonin

**Content**:
- Avoid phones, tablets, and computers for at least 60 minutes before bed
- Blue light suppresses melatonin production

**Visual Element**:
- Type: illustration
- Subject: Phone/tablet with "X" or prohibition sign
- Treatment: Bold crossed-out device icon, zigzag pattern accent

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "LIMIT SCREEN TIME"
- Labels: "60 min before bed", "Blue light blocks melatonin"

---

## Section 4: Cool, Dark Environment

**Key Concept**: Bedroom at 65-68°F with blackout curtains for optimal sleep

**Content**:
- Keep your bedroom at 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- Use blackout curtains and remove light sources

**Visual Element**:
- Type: illustration
- Subject: Thermometer showing ideal range, dark room
- Treatment: Cool-toned Memphis shapes, geometric bedroom scene

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "COOL & DARK ROOM"
- Labels: "65-68°F / 18-20°C", "Blackout curtains"

---

## Section 5: Watch What You Consume

**Key Concept**: Caffeine, heavy meals, and alcohol at wrong times disrupt sleep

**Content**:
- Avoid caffeine after 2 PM
- Avoid heavy meals within 3 hours of bedtime
- Alcohol disrupts REM sleep

**Visual Element**:
- Type: illustration
- Subject: Coffee cup and food with time markers
- Treatment: Bold food/drink icons with prohibition markers, pattern fills

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "WATCH WHAT YOU CONSUME"
- Labels: "No caffeine after 2 PM", "No heavy meals 3hrs before bed", "Alcohol disrupts REM"

---

## Section 6: Move Your Body

**Key Concept**: 30 minutes of daily exercise improves sleep, but not too close to bedtime

**Content**:
- Get at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily
- Finish workouts 3+ hours before sleep

**Visual Element**:
- Type: illustration
- Subject: Running/active figure
- Treatment: Dynamic geometric figure in motion, bold Memphis shapes

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "MOVE YOUR BODY"
- Labels: "30 min daily", "Finish 3+ hrs before bed"
