# THE GOLDEN AGE OF ART DECO

## Overview
A grand visual overview of the Art Deco movement (1920s-1930s) — its origins, defining characteristics, landmark architecture, design disciplines, iconic figures, and enduring legacy.

## Learning Objectives
The viewer will understand:
1. What defines Art Deco visually and culturally
2. The major architectural masterpieces and creative disciplines it shaped
3. Its historical arc from 1925 Paris to 1937 and its lasting influence

---

## Section 1: Origins (Hero Cell — 2x2)

**Key Concept**: Art Deco was born at the 1925 Paris Exposition, fusing Cubism, Futurism, and ancient art into bold geometric luxury.

**Content**:
- Born at the 1925 Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes in Paris
- Drew from Cubism, Futurism, and ancient Egyptian, Aztec, and African art
- Rejected Art Nouveau's flowing organic forms in favor of geometric abstraction and machine-age precision

**Visual Element**:
- Type: illustration
- Subject: A grand Art Deco archway or sunburst motif framing the title, with "PARIS 1925" prominently featured
- Treatment: Gold on black with radiating sunburst rays

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "BORN IN PARIS, 1925"
- Subhead: "Where Geometry Met Glamour"

## Section 2: Key Characteristics (1x2 tall cell)

**Key Concept**: Five defining pillars of the Art Deco aesthetic.

**Content**:
- Geometry: Sharp angles, zigzags, chevrons, sunbursts, stepped forms
- Materials: Chrome, glass, polished brass, lacquer, exotic woods
- Colors: Gold, black, silver, jewel tones (emerald, sapphire, ruby)
- Symmetry: Strong bilateral symmetry, centered compositions
- Luxury: Opulent surfaces, metallic sheens, rich ornamentation

**Visual Element**:
- Type: icon set
- Subject: Five geometric icons representing each pillar — zigzag, chrome circle, jewel, mirror axis, gold bar
- Treatment: Gold icons in vertical stack

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "THE 5 PILLARS"
- Labels: "GEOMETRY", "MATERIALS", "COLORS", "SYMMETRY", "LUXURY"

## Section 4: Design Disciplines (2x1 wide cell)

**Key Concept**: Art Deco permeated every creative field from fashion to industrial design.

**Content**:
- Interior Design: Geometric furniture, lacquered surfaces, mirrored walls
- Fashion: Coco Chanel, Jean Patou — angular silhouettes, beading, metallic fabrics
- Jewelry: Cartier, Van Cleef & Arpels — geometric gem settings, platinum, onyx
- Graphic Design: A.M. Cassandre — bold typography, streamlined travel posters
- Industrial Design: Streamlined trains, ocean liners, radios, clocks

**Visual Element**:
- Type: icon grid
- Subject: Five category icons — chair, dress, ring, poster, locomotive
- Treatment: Gold line drawings in geometric Art Deco style

## Section 6: Timeline (2x1 bottom strip)

**Key Concept**: Key milestones from 1925 to 1937.

**Content**:
- 1925: Paris Exposition launches Art Deco internationally
- 1927: The Jazz Singer — first "talkie" reflects Deco glamour
- 1929: Wall Street Crash — Deco persists through crisis
- 1930: Chrysler Building completed; briefly world's tallest
- 1931: Empire State Building completed
- 1933: Chicago Exposition showcases Streamline Moderne
- 1937: Paris Exposition — Art Deco's grand finale

**Visual Element**:
- Type: horizontal timeline
- Subject: Gold pillar dividers at each year, sunburst ornaments at key milestones
- Treatment: Gold pillars and fan ornaments on black band

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "THE ARC OF AN ERA"
- Labels: "1925", "1927", "1929", "1930", "1931", "1933", "1937"

## Section 7: Legacy (1x1 cell)

**Key Concept**: Art Deco's influence endures in modern design and culture.

**Content**:
- Over 300 Art Deco buildings in Miami Beach's Historic District — largest collection in the world
- Major revivals: 1960s-70s nostalgia, 1980s postmodern embrace, 2013 Great Gatsby film
- Persists in luxury branding and skyscraper aesthetics today

## Data Points (Verbatim)

### Statistics
- "1,046 feet tall" (Chrysler Building)
- "1,454 feet tall" (Empire State Building)
- "Over 300 Art Deco buildings in Miami Beach's Historic District"
- "largest collection of Art Deco architecture in the world"

### Key Terms
- **Art Deco**: From "Arts Décoratifs" — the 1925 Paris exposition
- **Streamline Moderne**: Late 1930s evolution emphasizing aerodynamic curves
- **Zigzag Moderne**: Early Art Deco's sharp angular phase
