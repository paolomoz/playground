# "Disneylandification" of the Brand Experience

## Overview
A strategic product philosophy that frames the distinction between generic discovery experiences and delightful brand-owned experiences. Users entering a brand expect Disneyland, not a search bar—and three pillars (actionable interactions, trust signals, meaningful events) plus a pipeline architecture make that possible.

## Learning Objectives
The viewer will understand:
1. The fundamental split between "discovery" (generic) and "brand experience" (delightful)
2. Three concrete pillars that make brand experience exceptional
3. The pipeline architecture: capture events → build context → deliver personalised experience

## Section 4: Pillar 3 — Meaningful Events

**Key Concept**: Track events that build user context—the raw material for exceptional experience.

**Content**:
- "Meaningful events -- track events related to the user that are useful to build context."
- "Context is the raw material for exceptional experience. Without it, personalisation is guesswork."
- Candidates: "purchase history, content engagement patterns, support interactions, lifecycle stage, expressed preferences vs. revealed preferences"

**Visual Element**:
- Type: collection/stream illustration
- Subject: Stream of event signals (purchase, engagement, support, lifecycle) flowing into a context container
- Treatment: Shows raw events being collected and refined into intelligence

**Text Labels**:
- Headline: "Meaningful Events"
- Subhead: "Context is the raw material"
- Labels: "Purchase history", "Engagement patterns", "Support interactions", "Lifecycle stage", "Expressed vs. revealed preferences"
- Footer: "Without context, personalisation is guesswork"
