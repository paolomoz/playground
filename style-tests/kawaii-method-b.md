# kawaii

Round-faced characters with enormous sparkling eyes, rosy cheek blushes, and tiny dot mouths beaming from a pastel dreamscape of soft pinks, lavenders, and mint greens — every object anthropomorphized with a happy face, every corner rounded into a gentle pillow shape, every surface dusted with tiny stars and hearts floating in the background. This is Sanrio's Hello Kitty universe, Sumikko Gurashi's shy corner creatures, Pusheen the cat rolling in contentment — a visual language where even a coffee cup has eyelashes and a toaster wears a bow. Aggressively adorable, deliberately infantile, emotionally warm.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Soft pink (#FFB6C1), baby lavender (#C3B1E1), mint green (#98D8C8)
- Background: Cream white (#FFF8F0), cotton candy pink (#FFE4EC), pale sky blue (#E0F0FF), warm vanilla (#FFF5E6)
- Accents: Peach (#FFCBA4), butter yellow (#FFF3B0), coral (#FF8A80), periwinkle (#CCCCFF), seafoam (#B2DFDB)
- Darks: Soft charcoal (#4A4A4A) for outlines — NEVER true black, always slightly warm and gentle
- Cheek blush: Rosy pink (#FF9AA2) — the signature kawaii element, two soft oval spots on every face
- Sparkle: White (#FFFFFF) for star highlights in eyes and decorative sparkle accents
- Text: Soft charcoal (#4A4A4A) or warm dark brown (#5D4037) — never harsh black

## Variants

| Variant | Focus | Visual Emphasis |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| **Chibi** | Small round-bodied characters with oversized heads, maximum cute proportions | Heads 50-60% of total body height, stubby limbs, enormous eyes with star highlights, minimal body detail, characters front-and-center as the primary visual element |
| **Food** | Anthropomorphized food items with happy faces and accessories | Smiling rice balls with tiny blush cheeks, happy dumplings with sparkle eyes, a contented bowl of ramen with a little bow — every food item alive and delighted to exist |
| **Sticker** | Individual characters/items with white outline border, designed as die-cut sticker shapes | Each element has a thick white outline border creating a "sticker" cutout feel, arranged on a background as if placed by hand, slight overlap, collectible energy |
| **Scene** | Full environment with kawaii characters interacting in a pastel world | Rounded buildings with smiling windows, puffy clouds with faces, flowers with happy expressions, characters inhabiting a fully anthropomorphized gentle landscape |

## Visual Elements

- **Enormous sparkling eyes** — large circular or oval eyes taking up 30-40% of the face area, with large white highlight spots (star-shaped or circular) creating a dewy, glassy, alive look. Two or three highlight spots per eye. This is THE defining kawaii feature
- **Rosy cheek blushes** — two soft oval or circular pink spots on the cheeks of every character and anthropomorphized object, conveying warmth, shyness, and emotional softness. Blush is NOT optional
- **Tiny simple mouths** — small dot, gentle curve, or cat-mouth (w-shape) positioned low on the face. Mouths are minimal — expression comes from the eyes and blush, not from elaborate mouth shapes
- **Oversized heads on small bodies** — head-to-body ratio of approximately 1:1 to 1:1.5 (massively disproportionate). Stubby little limbs, rounded torso, no neck or very short neck
- **Everything anthropomorphized** — cups, clouds, stars, food items, electronics, buildings ALL get faces (eyes + blush + tiny mouth). Nothing is inanimate in kawaii world
- **Rounded corners on everything** — no sharp edges anywhere in the composition. Rectangles become rounded rectangles, triangles get soft vertices, even straight lines have slightly soft terminals
- **Floating decorative elements** — tiny stars, hearts, sparkles, music notes, small flowers, and dots floating in the background space around characters, suggesting an ambient atmosphere of happiness
- **Pastel color fills** — all surfaces filled with soft, slightly desaturated pastel tones. NO bold saturated colors, NO dark heavy fills. Everything gentle, light, and warm
- **Thick soft outlines** — character outlines in warm charcoal (not black), approximately 2-3px, with consistently rounded line caps and joins. The outline feels soft, not sharp
- **Hair bows, ribbons, and small accessories** — characters frequently wear a single identifying accessory: a bow, a flower, a tiny hat, a star clip. Simple and iconic
- **Soft drop shadows** — very subtle, warm-toned shadows (not gray, but slightly pink or peach) beneath characters, giving gentle floating-above-surface feeling
- **NO sharp angles, NO dark colors, NO realistic proportions** — anything angular, dark, or anatomically correct breaks the kawaii spell
- **NO complex detailed backgrounds** — backgrounds are simple: solid pastel, gentle gradient, or minimal pattern (tiny polka dots, small hearts). Background never competes with characters

## Compositional Patterns

| Content Structure | Composition | Reference |
|-------------------|-------------|-----------|
| Hierarchy/levels | Largest/most important character centered with star sparkles, smaller supporting characters arranged around in a gentle arc, size = importance | Main character center stage, friends gathered around admiringly |
| Flow/process | Characters shown in sequence left-to-right, each performing the next step, connected by dotted lines with hearts or stars as waypoints | Cooking sequence: character mixing, then baking, then decorating, each step adorable |
| Connections | Characters holding hands, linked by decorative chains of hearts or stars, or grouped in a cuddle pile | Friend group connected by a garland of tiny flowers and hearts |
| Cycles | Characters arranged in a circle facing inward, a heart or star at the center, dotted circular path connecting them | Seasonal cycle: spring bunny, summer bear, autumn fox, winter penguin in a ring |
| Comparison | Two characters side by side with different expressions or accessories, gentle visual distinction without conflict | Happy cat vs. sleepy cat, both adorable, distinguished by eye shape and accessory |
| Categories | Different character species or food types grouped by type, each group on its own pastel background cloud | Animal friends: bunny group on pink, cat group on lavender, bear group on mint |
| Growth | Small seed/baby character on left growing progressively larger and more accessorized moving right, sparkle effects increasing | Tiny sprout becoming a smiling flower with a bow, getting happier at each stage |
| Collection | Grid of individual characters/items each in their own rounded frame, sticker-sheet arrangement, each slightly different | Sticker sheet of 12 different kawaii food items, each with unique face and accessory |

## Typography

- Rounded sans-serif faces: Quicksand, Nunito, Varela Round, or similar — every letterform with soft rounded terminals
- Lowercase preference for body text — feels gentle and approachable
- Uppercase acceptable for short titles if the font is sufficiently rounded and soft
- Text in warm charcoal or dark brown, never true black — maintaining the gentle overall tone
- Letters can be slightly irregular in size or baseline for hand-lettered charm
- Key words can be in pastel pink or lavender for emphasis, or inside small rounded speech bubbles
- Exclamation marks and question marks can be drawn with small hearts or stars as their dots
- Emoji-like decorative characters (hearts, stars, musical notes) can be mixed into text
- NO angular fonts, NO serif fonts, NO condensed or aggressive type — everything must feel huggable
- Title text can have a subtle pastel outline or soft shadow for sticker-like effect

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT anime/manga** — anime has varied proportions, dramatic poses, detailed clothing, action scenes, and complex narratives. Kawaii is simple, round, static, cute, and emotionally one-note (happy). No dynamic action poses, no detailed hair styles, no dramatic shading.
- **NOT chibi anime** — while chibi shares the big-head proportion, chibi anime characters retain detailed anime features (complex eyes, detailed outfits, expressive faces). Kawaii faces are MUCH simpler: dot eyes or simple circles, tiny mouths, minimal features.
- **NOT Pixar/Disney 3D** — 3D rendered cuteness with subsurface scattering, complex lighting, and realistic proportions is a different aesthetic. Kawaii is flat or very subtly dimensional, outlined, pastel.
- **NOT pastel style** (the generic pastel infographic) — pastel infographics use soft colors but with normal infographic structures (charts, icons, layouts). Kawaii requires anthropomorphized characters with faces on everything, not just pastel-colored standard graphics.
- **NOT emoji** — emojis are standardized, geometric, yellow-circle-based. Kawaii is more organic, rounded, pastel, with blush cheeks and sparkle eyes that emojis lack.
- **NOT Memphis or pop art** — those styles are bold, loud, clashing, energetic. Kawaii is gentle, soft, harmonious, and never confrontational.
- **NOT realistic illustration with cute subjects** — a realistically rendered kitten is not kawaii. Kawaii requires stylistic simplification: enormous eyes, tiny mouths, rounded everything, pastel palette.
- **NOT just pink** — kawaii uses a RANGE of pastels (lavender, mint, peach, butter yellow, sky blue). All-pink is Barbie, not kawaii. The palette must include at least 3-4 different pastel hues.
- This must look like a Sanrio product, a Sumikko Gurashi merchandise design, or a LINE Friends sticker sheet — soft, round, pastel, blushing, sparkling, with faces on everything and not a sharp corner in sight.

## Best For

Children's educational content, wellness and self-care topics, cooking and recipe guides, pet care information, gentle onboarding tutorials, product features for consumer apps, gift guides, seasonal celebrations, any content that benefits from emotional warmth, approachable non-threatening presentation, and the universal appeal of things that are impossibly, aggressively, irresistibly cute.
