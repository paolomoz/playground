# atomic-age

Flat, stylized mid-century modern illustration bursting with atomic optimism — turquoise and orange shapes floating on warm cream backgrounds, starburst wall clocks ticking with multi-pointed rays, boomerang coffee tables hovering on tapered splayed legs, and jaunty little figures with pointy shoes striding confidently into the Space Age. This is a 1955 Golden Book illustration, a Googie diner placemat, a Charley Harper wildlife print, a Saul Bass title sequence — playful, confident, clean, and irresistibly retro. Every surface is flat matte color with no gradients, every shape sits on impossibly thin tapered legs, every accent is a radiating starburst or an orbiting electron.

## Visual Elements

- **Starburst / atomic star shapes**: sharp multi-pointed stars radiating from a center point, like a sparkle or a retro wall clock with long and short alternating rays — THE single most iconic mid-century motif, used as decorative accents, bullet points, and section markers
- **Boomerang and kidney-bean shapes**: asymmetric curved forms used as dividers, containers, and decorative accents — organic but geometric, like a coffee table or amoeba viewed from above, the quintessential MCM organic shape
- **Tapered legs and atomic supports**: thin angled lines tapering to sharp points, holding up shapes like mid-century furniture legs — EVERYTHING hovers slightly off the ground on impossibly thin splayed supports
- **Electron orbit paths**: thin elliptical rings orbiting around central circles, suggesting friendly atomic models — science made decorative, approachable, and optimistic
- **Diamond / rhombus grid patterns**: tilted squares repeating as background texture or accent pattern, like a 1950s kitchen tile backsplash in contrasting colors
- **Flat color fills with ZERO gradients**: each shape is a single solid matte color, cleanly bounded by consistent outline — the flat illustration look is absolutely essential, NO shading, NO dimension
- **Simplified figures with exaggerated proportions**: people rendered as stylized shapes — small round heads, triangular torsos, long thin limbs, pointy shoes — charming and jaunty, striding confidently, NOT realistic
- **Googie swooping angles**: architectural roof shapes that swoop dramatically upward at the tips, arrow-like pointers, boomerang curves all suggesting forward motion and jet-age velocity
- **Thin consistent outline strokes in charcoal**: shapes outlined with uniform thin dark lines, sometimes slightly offset from the fill color, giving a hand-drawn screen-print registration quality
- **Warm paper texture grain**: subtle fibrous grain visible through the cream background and faintly through color fills, like risograph printing on textured cardstock
- **Abstract botanical shorthand**: simple leaf forms (ovals on sticks), circles-on-stems suggesting lollipop trees or dandelions — nature reduced to pure geometric essence
- **NO photorealism, NO gradients, NO drop shadows, NO glossy effects, NO 3D rendering** — everything is FLAT, GRAPHIC, PRINTED, and MATTE

## Typography

- Clean sans-serif with personality — slightly rounded letterforms suggesting warmth and approachability, not sterile geometric perfection
- Playful baseline variation: letters can bounce slightly on the baseline, creating friendly rhythm without looking sloppy or childish
- Mixed case preferred for body text — lowercase feels more mid-century-modern approachable than corporate all-caps
- Titles can use display-weight condensed sans-serif with tight tracking — bold and confident but never heavy
- Script or casual brush lettering for single accent words — one word in casual hand-lettered script adds warmth (use very sparingly)
- Text often placed at jaunty angles, tilted 5-10 degrees off horizontal — cheerful informality, not rigid precision
- Color-coded labels matching their associated shapes — turquoise label for turquoise shape, orange label for orange shape
- NO formal serif faces, NO blackletter, NO heavy decorative Victorian type — everything is airy, light, modern, and friendly
- Letter-spacing can be generous in labels (playful breathing room) or tight in headlines (confident boldness)

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT a vintage photograph with retro filter** — this is FLAT STYLIZED ILLUSTRATION, not photography. No photographic textures, no film grain simulation, no sepia filtering over realistic images
- **NOT pixel art or 8-bit** — mid-century modern uses SMOOTH curves and clean vector-like shapes, not pixelated grid forms. No jagged pixel edges
- **NOT corporate Memphis (the modern Silicon Valley style)** — though both use flat illustration, atomic age has a SPECIFIC warm retro palette (turquoise, orange, mustard), starburst motifs, tapered furniture legs, and Googie architectural forms that corporate Memphis completely lacks. Corporate Memphis uses pastel purples, squishy blob people, and geometric gradients — all wrong for MCM
- **NOT psychedelic 1960s hippie** — atomic age is CLEAN, OPTIMISTIC, and STRUCTURED with precise geometric shapes. Psychedelic is swirly, chaotic, melting, and hallucinogenic. No tie-dye, no liquid lettering, no mushrooms
- **NOT Art Deco** — no gold, no black-and-gold metallic sheen, no ziggurats, no sunburst fan rays, no stepped pyramid forms. Atomic age is WARM, COLORFUL, PLAYFUL where Art Deco is formal, opulent, and severe
- **NOT Memphis Design (1980s postmodern)** — though both are colorful and flat, Memphis uses HOT PINK, ZIGZAGS, TERRAZZO SPECKLE, and deliberately clashing patterns with thick black outlines. Atomic age uses WARM EARTH TONES (turquoise, orange, cream), STARBURSTS, BOOMERANGS, and TAPERED LEGS. Completely different vocabularies
- **NOT airline-travel-poster** — though both are mid-century, airline posters have gradient skies, romantic atmosphere, and destination landmarks. Atomic age is FLAT, GRAPHIC, and PATTERN-BASED with starburst decorations, not atmospheric
- **NOT a generic flat vector illustration** — the SPECIFIC vocabulary of starbursts, boomerangs, tapered legs, electron orbits, and Googie architecture is what makes it atomic age. Remove those motifs and you just have a flat illustration
- This must look like a page from a 1955 Golden Book science primer or a Charley Harper wildlife print — flat, warm, optimistic, with starbursts sparkling and boomerangs swooping and everything perched on impossibly thin tapered legs — not a modern flat illustration that happens to use retro colors
