# constructivism

Aggressive diagonal compositions slashing across the frame in bold red and black — massive wedge-shaped triangles driving through white space, stark photomontage figures overlaid with blocky sans-serif typography, industrial gears and factory smokestacks rendered as flat graphic shapes. This is Lissitzky's red wedge beating the whites, Rodchenko's workers reaching upward in photomontage, Stepanova's athletes as geometric diagrams of motion — revolutionary graphic design stripped to its most urgent, most powerful, most structurally dynamic form.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Revolutionary red (#CC0000), dense matte black (#1A1A1A), stark paper white (#F5F0E8)
- Secondary: Industrial steel gray (#6B6B6B), warm newsprint cream (#E8DFD0)
- Accents: Deep navy (#1A2744), brick red-orange (#C04420), olive drab (#4A5A2B) — used sparingly, never more than one accent per composition
- Text: Black on white/cream, or reversed white on red/black — text as structural element, not decoration
- Gold/metallics: NEVER used. This is proletarian graphic design, not gilded ornamentation.

### Palette Combinations

| Combination | Colors |
|-------------|--------|
| Lissitzky | Revolutionary red + black + white — the classic triad, maximum contrast, red wedge energy cutting through white space |
| Rodchenko | Red + steel gray + cream — photomontage palette, industrial grit, newspaper texture |
| Propaganda | Red + black + deep navy — dense, authoritative, maximum graphic weight |
| Industrial | Black + steel gray + cream + brick red-orange accent — factory aesthetic, machinery, structural engineering |

## Variants

| Variant | Focus | Visual Emphasis |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| **Agitprop** | Bold propaganda poster energy | Massive diagonal red triangles piercing through the composition, oversized sans-serif slogans, photomontage workers with outstretched arms, megaphones, radiating lines from central action |
| **Typographic** | Letters as architecture | Words stacked, rotated, scaled as structural beams and girders, text at 45-degree and 90-degree angles forming the entire composition, letterforms as building blocks |
| **Photomontage** | Cut-and-paste layered imagery | Figures cut from photographs at sharp angles, overlaid on geometric color fields, scale distortions (giant hand over tiny factory), layered paper edges visible |
| **Structural** | Engineering and construction diagrams | Scaffolding, cranes, factory cross-sections rendered as flat geometric plans, axonometric projections, technical callout lines pointing to components |

## Visual Elements

- Massive sharp-edged red triangles (wedges) slashing diagonally across the composition, piercing through other elements — the dominant motif
- Bold black bars and beams running at aggressive 30-45 degree diagonal angles, forming structural scaffolding across the frame
- Flat circular forms — gears, wheels, megaphone cones — rendered as pure geometric cutouts, no shading, no dimension
- Figures as photomontage cutouts: workers with outstretched arms, clenched fists raised skyward, athletes in motion — silhouetted or high-contrast
- Radiating lines bursting outward from a central point of action, like a shout or explosion made visible
- Blocky sans-serif typography integrated as structural elements — words become beams, titles become walls, letters become architecture
- Extreme scale contrasts: a giant hand reaching over a tiny factory, a massive eye overlooking a miniature crowd
- Paper-white negative space used aggressively — open areas are not empty, they are structural voids that the red wedges slash through
- Industrial motifs: factory smokestacks, construction cranes, gears, radio towers — all rendered as flat graphic cutouts
- Thick black outlines and borders around key elements, creating sharp separation between forms
- Circular target/bullseye forms with radiating concentric rings, often in red and black
- Overlapping geometric planes with visible hard edges — no blending, no gradients, every boundary crisp and deliberate
- Arrows and directional indicators — bold, angular, pointing toward the action or the future

## Typography

- Heavy blocky sans-serif, industrial and structural — imagine steel I-beams cast as letterforms
- ALL CAPS dominant, lowercase only for secondary body text
- Text placed at aggressive diagonal angles (30, 45, 90 degrees), never casually tilted — every angle is deliberate and structural
- Words used as architectural elements — a title becomes a beam, a label becomes a wall, typography IS the structure
- Extreme size hierarchy: headlines 10x the size of body text, massive single words dominating entire composition zones
- Red text on white, white text on red, black text on cream — always maximum contrast, never decorative color
- Text can overlap geometric forms, partially hidden behind red wedges or black bars
- Letters spaced wide or compressed tight for structural effect — tracking as a compositional tool
- NO serif faces, NO script, NO decorative fonts — pure functional geometry
- Numbers rendered oversized and bold, like industrial serial numbers or production statistics

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT Bauhaus** — Bauhaus uses warm cream grounds, luminous colored halos around circles, and musical compositional flow. Constructivism is harsher: cold white space, aggressive red wedges, industrial structural diagrams. Bauhaus is art school; Constructivism is factory floor.
- **NOT Futurism** — Futurism dissolves forms into motion blur and speed lines. Constructivism keeps forms sharp, hard-edged, and structurally solid. Nothing blurs, nothing dissolves — every edge is a knife cut.
- **NOT Art Deco** — Art Deco is symmetrical luxury, gold leaf, ornamental sunbursts, and geometric elegance. Constructivism rejects ornament entirely. No curves for beauty, no decoration, no embellishment.
- **NOT Soviet Socialist Realism** — No realistic painted figures, no heroic muscular portraits, no golden wheat fields. Constructivism is graphic and flat, not painterly and dimensional.
- **NOT generic "red and black"** — A composition using red and black is not automatically Constructivist. The essence is the aggressive diagonal structural composition, the wedge forms, the photomontage layering, the typography-as-architecture.
- **NOT balanced or symmetrical** — Compositions must feel dynamic and off-kilter, with aggressive diagonal thrust. Centered, symmetrical layouts kill the revolutionary energy.
- **NOT soft or rounded** — No rounded corners, no soft gradients, no gentle curves. Every form is hard-edged, sharp-cornered, structurally rigid.
- This must look like Lissitzky's propaganda posters and Rodchenko's photomontages — aggressive red wedges slashing through white space, photomontage figures at dramatic scales, blocky typography as structural architecture — not a generic corporate infographic that happens to use red.

## Best For

Political and organizational messaging, revolutionary or transformative narratives, industrial processes, manufacturing workflows, structural engineering topics, bold announcements, campaign launches, protest and advocacy, any content that demands maximum graphic urgency, aggressive visual hierarchy, and unapologetic structural boldness.
