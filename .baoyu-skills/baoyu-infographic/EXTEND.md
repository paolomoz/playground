# Project Preferences

## Custom Styles

Additional style definitions are in `.baoyu-skills/baoyu-infographic/styles/`.
When a requested style is not found in `references/styles/`, check this directory.

Available custom styles:
- `ukiyo-e` — Japanese woodblock print aesthetic (Edo period)
- `art-nouveau` — Flowing organic lines, natural forms, elegant ornamentation (1890-1910)
- `bauhaus` — Systematic geometric design, primary colors, functional clarity (1919-1933)
- `sumi-e` — Black ink brush strokes on rice paper, misty landscapes, zen minimalism
- `adobe` — Vibrant creative platform aesthetic, bold Adobe Clean headlines, card layouts, electric blue and hot pink accents
- `pastel` — Soft pastel gradients (5 rotating palettes), floating rounded cards, holographic 3D stars, playful squiggles, modern Adobe Express aesthetic
- `art-deco` — Opulent 1920s-30s geometric glamour, gold-on-black, sunbursts, ziggurats, fan motifs, jewel-tone accents
- `atomic-age` — Mid-century modern illustration, 1950s atomic optimism, starbursts, boomerangs, turquoise/orange/mustard on cream
- `memphis` — 1980s Memphis Group postmodern design, bold geometric patterns, zigzags, squiggles, terrazzo confetti, hot pink/electric blue/acid yellow, thick black outlines, deliberately clashing
- `matsumoto` — Taiyo Matsumoto-inspired loose expressive ink, urban chaos, tangled wires, dense Japanese alleys, sketchy crosshatching, watercolor washes, raw and kinetic
